# Qalculus Research

Qalculus Research is the long-horizon part of Qalculus Labs. It gives us room to
follow questions that are mathematically worthwhile even when they do not fit a
short product schedule.

## Public direction

Our broad areas of interest are:

- applied mathematical systems;
- theoretical mathematics and rigorous computation; and
- quantum and emerging computational methods.

These headings describe a direction, not a list of finished projects (but will).

## How we want to work

We want research to be ambitious. A serious project
should state its assumptions, compare itself with the right prior work, define
what would count as failure.

Applied work should eventually meet a real system or a credible benchmark.
Theoretical work does not need to become a feature to be valuable. Work in
quantum or emerging computation should not become an advantage without a fair
comparison and complete resource accounting.

## Publication plan

When a project is ready, we may publish a paper or preprint, a reproducible
benchmark, an approved synthetic demonstration, or a plain-language research
note.
