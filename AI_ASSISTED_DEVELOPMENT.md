# AI-assisted development

AI tools have helped with parts of the Qalculus Labs work, including drafts of
code, tests, documentation, interface copy, and possible engineering
approaches. We disclose that because it is part of how the project is being
built.

AI assistance is not treated as proof that something is correct. The project
owner remains responsible for the architecture, mathematics, source review,
testing, security, licensing, and every public claim.

In practice, that means:

- generated code must be reviewed and tested before it is accepted;
- mathematical claims need explicit assumptions and appropriate checks;
- public claims need repository, benchmark, or runtime evidence;
- dependencies and generated assets need licence and provenance review; and
- secrets, private data, and unpublished sensitive research must stay out of
  public prompts and artifacts.

AI can produce convincing mistakes as easily as convincing prose. We use it as
a tool for exploration and implementation, while keeping human judgement and
accountability at the centre of the work.
